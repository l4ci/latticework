# Example: inversion

**You invoke it with:**
> "Invert our goal of keeping our best engineers"

**It needs from you:**
- GOAL (required): "Keep our strongest engineers (the top ~15% the org depends on) for the next three years."
- CONTEXT (optional): "Series-B startup, ~40 engineers, post-hypergrowth slowdown, two strong engineers left last quarter; compensation is roughly market; the founders worry about a slow drain rather than a sudden exodus."

**You get back:** a saved markdown report (`inversion-engineer-retention-<date>.md`) with the goal stated forward and inverted ("how would we guarantee they all quit?"), a ranked table of failure paths across five lenses with plausibility and damage scores, the absolute "never" bright lines, an avoid-list mapping each top failure path to the constraint or move that neutralizes it, and a verdict on what avoiding those failures changes about how the team is run.

**See a full worked example:** [inversion-engineer-retention-2026-06-19.md](inversion-engineer-retention-2026-06-19.md), the report this invocation produces.
