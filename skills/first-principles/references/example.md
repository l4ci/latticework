# Example: first-principles

**You invoke it with:**
> "We run a 24/7 staffed support desk because that is what support means. Reason it from first principles."

**It needs from you:**
- PROBLEM (required): "How should our SaaS product handle customer problems outside business hours?"
- CONVENTIONAL APPROACH (required): "A support desk staffed by humans around the clock, three shifts, because real support means a person is always reachable. Embedded assumptions: a human must answer; coverage must be continuous; the desk must be staffed by us; nighttime volume justifies live staff."
- GOAL (optional): "Resolve customer problems fast enough that customers stay, without the cost of round-the-clock staffing sinking the unit economics."

**You get back:** a saved markdown report (`first-principles-support-desk-<date>.md`) with the conventional approach and its assumptions, a teardown table marking each assumption inherited or necessary, the bedrock truths that survive (each flagged fact or assumption), a reconstruction built from the bedrock, a side-by-side contrast of convention versus the first-principles answer, and a verdict on whether the round-the-clock staffed desk is load-bearing or inherited.

**See a full worked example:** [first-principles-support-desk-2026-06-19.md](first-principles-support-desk-2026-06-19.md): the report this invocation produces.
