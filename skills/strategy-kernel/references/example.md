# Example: strategy-kernel

**You invoke it with:**
> "Build a strategy for our analytics SaaS, we're losing customers to a free competitor"

**It needs from you:**
- ORGANIZATION (required): "Tessellate, a mid-size SaaS firm selling product-analytics software to growth-stage tech companies."
- CHALLENGE (required): "A well-funded competitor launched a free tier that covers most of what mid-market teams use us for, and our net revenue retention is sliding. What is at stake: the renewal base that the whole company runs on."
- EXISTING STRATEGY (optional): "Our strategy is to be the leading customer-obsessed analytics platform, grow ARR 40% next year, and win on product velocity."
- CONTEXT (optional): "The free competitor is venture-subsidized; our strength is deep workflows for data teams; most churn is at the low end where the free tier bites; enterprise accounts are sticky but few."

**You get back:** a saved markdown report (`strategy-kernel-tessellate-<date>.md`) with the diagnosis and crux, a guiding policy that makes a hard choice and states what it rules out, three to six coherent actions with a coherence check, a bad-strategy audit of both the new kernel and the existing statement against Rumelt's four hallmarks, and a verdict on whether the result is a real strategy.

**See a full worked example:** [strategy-kernel-tessellate-2026-06-19.md](strategy-kernel-tessellate-2026-06-19.md), the report this invocation produces.
