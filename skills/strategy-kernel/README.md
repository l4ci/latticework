# strategy-kernel

## What this is

A strategy, in Richard Rumelt's *Good Strategy / Bad Strategy* (2011), is not a vision, a goal, or a list of priorities. It is a way of dealing with a specific challenge, and it has a hard core he calls the **kernel**, three parts that must hold together. The **diagnosis** names the crux: the one obstacle that, out of a thousand facts, is doing the work. The **guiding policy** is the directional approach aimed at that crux, a signpost and a guardrail that makes a hard choice and rules things out. The **coherent action** is the handful of coordinated commitments that carry the policy out, reinforcing one another rather than fighting. Against this, Rumelt sets four hallmarks of bad strategy: fluff, failure to face the challenge, mistaking goals for strategy, and bad objectives (a scramble of a list, or blue-sky goals that just restate the problem).

The kernel, the crux, the sources of power, and the four hallmarks live in [references/strategy-kernel.md](references/strategy-kernel.md).

## What it does

The skill runs in stages. It captures the organization, the challenge, what is at stake, and any existing "strategy" to critique. Then it fans out four diagnostician subagents in parallel, each naming the crux from a different angle (competitive, internal capability, structural, customer), so the diagnosis is chosen rather than stumbled into. A synthesis pass picks or merges the sharpest diagnosis, crafts a guiding policy that makes a hard choice and states what it rules out, designs three to six coherent actions with a coherence check, and then audits the whole result against the four hallmarks, fixing what fails. The output is a saved markdown report with the diagnosis, the guiding policy, the coherent action, the bad-strategy audit, and a verdict.

One rule keeps it honest: a goal is not a strategy. The audit cuts fluff, catches goals smuggled in where a method belongs, and rewrites any objective that just restates the challenge.

## When to use it

Reach for the kernel to build a strategy for an organization, unit, product, or initiative, or to test whether something already called a strategy actually is one. It decides *what the strategy is*; pair it with sharper tools when the question is narrower:

- For a broad qualitative read of position before strategy, use [swot-analysis](../swot-analysis/).
- For industry profit structure and competitive pressure specifically, use [porters-five-forces](../porters-five-forces/).
- To break out of head-to-head competition by redefining the market, use [blue-ocean-strategy](../blue-ocean-strategy/).
- To deploy a chosen strategy through the organization, use [hoshin-kanri](../hoshin-kanri/).
- To turn a strategy into measurable near-term commitments, use [okr](../okr/).
