# business-model-canvas

## What this is

The Business Model Canvas lays a business model out on one page to show how an organization creates, delivers, and captures value. It comes from Alexander Osterwalder and Yves Pigneur (*Business Model Generation*, 2010), building on Osterwalder's doctoral work. The canvas has nine building blocks: Customer Segments, Value Propositions, Channels, Customer Relationships, and Revenue Streams on the right side, where value reaches the customer; Key Resources, Key Activities, Key Partnerships, and Cost Structure on the left, where the value gets produced. Value Propositions sits in the middle and joins the two halves. The point is not to fill nine boxes but to test whether they fit each other.

The nine blocks, their key questions, the right-side/left-side/viability logic, and the pitfalls are in [references/canvas.md](references/canvas.md).

## What it does

The skill runs the analysis in parallel. Nine analyst subagents each take one block, describe it for the specific business with real evidence, and mark every claim a fact or an assumption still to be tested. A synthesis pass then checks whether the blocks fit: whether the value propositions match the segments, whether channels and relationships suit each segment, and whether the revenue streams plausibly outrun the cost structure. It tells the model as one story and points to the weakest links. The output is a saved markdown report with the canvas laid out block by block, a verdict on coherence and viability, and the riskiest assumptions called out. Two rules keep it honest: each block separates fact from assumption so a young model's real risk is not buried, and the canvas stays a model of the business, not a plan for it, with no forecasts or roadmaps.

## When to use it

Reach for this skill to map a business model on one page, understand how an existing business works, or pressure-test a new venture's model. The unit is one defined business model, not a whole company's strategy and not a business plan.

- If the question narrows to whether one offer matches one customer segment, use [value-proposition-canvas](../value-proposition-canvas/), which zooms into two of these nine blocks.
- If you are testing the riskiest assumption with a live experiment, use [lean-startup](../lean-startup/).
- If you need a broad qualitative read on competitive position rather than the model itself, use [swot-analysis](../swot-analysis/).

**You provide:** one defined business, product, or venture to model (stage and market context are optional and shape which assumptions matter). Something like "map the business model canvas for our B2B invoicing SaaS."
