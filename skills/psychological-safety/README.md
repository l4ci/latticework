# psychological-safety

## What this is

Psychological safety is the shared belief, held by members of a team, that the team is safe for interpersonal risk-taking: speaking up with an idea, a question, a concern, a mistake, or a challenge without fear of being punished, dismissed, or humiliated. The construct comes from Amy Edmondson (*The Fearless Organization*, 2019, building on her 1999 research). It is a property of the team climate, not a trait of any individual, and it is one of the strongest predictors of team learning and, through learning, of performance. Safety is not the same as comfort. The target is high safety paired with high accountability, what Edmondson calls the learning zone.

The behavioral markers, the safety-by-accountability 2x2, the three leader practices, and the pitfalls live in [references/psych-safety.md](references/psych-safety.md).

## What it does

The skill runs the diagnosis in parallel. Five assessor subagents each take one dimension of the climate (voice and speaking up, response to mistakes, help-seeking, inclusion of difference, and risk-taking and challenge) and score it against Edmondson's seven markers with real evidence, judging the team rather than the people in it. A synthesis pass then plots the team on the safety-by-accountability 2x2, names the weakest dimensions and the behaviors driving them, and prescribes against Edmondson's three leader practices: frame the work as a learning problem, acknowledge your own fallibility, and model curiosity by asking questions. The output is a saved markdown report with the climate read dimension by dimension, the 2x2 placement, the prescription, and leading indicators to watch.

Three guardrails keep it honest. It treats safety as a climate read from observable behavior, never a label on an individual; it targets candor under challenge, since a pleasant, agreeable team can be the least safe; and it never lets a prescription trade away the standard, because lowering the bar in the name of safety lands the team in the comfort zone rather than making it safe.

## When to use it

Reach for this when learning or innovation is stalling, people are not speaking up, mistakes get hidden, or there has just been a failure or near-miss, and you want to know why and what to do. The unit is a single team with a leader and recurring interaction, not a whole company and not an individual. Pick a sibling when the question is different:

- If you want the full team-effectiveness stack with trust as its base, use [five-dysfunctions-of-a-team](../five-dysfunctions-of-a-team/).
- If you want to place the team on its developmental arc, use [tuckman](../tuckman/).
- If the question is one individual's threat response in a moment, use [scarf](../scarf/).
