# Example invocation

## You invoke it with

> "Tighten this release email, it sounds like a robot wrote it. It goes to existing customers."

Or, in write mode:

> "Write me a short release note for customers: imports are faster and large files no longer fail. Plain, no hype."

## It needs from you

- **The text** (edit mode) or **the brief** (write mode): the draft to cut, or what the text must say.
- **Purpose and reader**: what the text is for and who reads it. The example gives both: a release email, to existing customers. This is the anchor; without it the skill cannot judge "long enough" and will ask one short question.
- Optional: a length ceiling, the register or voice to hold, or a sample of your own writing to match.

## You get back

- The tightened text, ready to send, with no AI tells and no em dashes.
- A one-line before/after word count when you handed it existing text.
- A short note on what did the most work: the whole sections dropped and the tells rewritten, not a line-by-line diff.

For a long piece, it also saves the final text and the change note to `concise-writing-<subject-slug>-<YYYY-MM-DD>.md`.
