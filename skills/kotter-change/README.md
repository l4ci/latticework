# kotter-change

## What this is

Kotter's 8-step model (John Kotter, *Leading Change*, 1996) is a method for leading large-scale organizational change through eight roughly sequential steps: create a sense of urgency, build a guiding coalition, form a strategic vision, communicate it to enlist a volunteer army, remove the barriers in the way, generate short-term wins, sustain acceleration, and anchor the change in the culture. The discipline is that change is led, not announced. You build the conditions for it before you push, prove it works before you scale, and anchor it before you let go. Skipping a step buys apparent speed and then breaks the change later. Kotter revisited the model in *Accelerate* (2014), reframing the steps as concurrent, continuous accelerators run by a broad volunteer army alongside the management hierarchy, but the eight ideas stay the same.

The eight steps, their gate questions, the Accelerate update, and the pitfalls live in [references/kotter.md](references/kotter.md).

## What it does

The skill facilitates the eight steps with a gate check on each one, holding a step until it answers its question (urgency felt, coalition powerful, vision clear, vision communicated, barriers removed, wins delivered, acceleration sustained, change anchored) before the next begins. Two steps spread the work across parallel subagents: step 1 surfaces urgency evidence from several angles at once (market, customer, financial, competitive, operational), and step 5 inventories barriers across lenses (structure, systems and incentives, skills, people). The findings are pooled and judged with you while the order across steps stays fixed. The output is a saved markdown change plan covering all eight steps plus a record of the gates.

The gates guard against the common ways change fails: skipping urgency so people never give the effort it takes, declaring victory at the first short-term win so momentum collapses while the change is still shallow, and never anchoring it in culture or in who gets promoted so the organization reverts the moment the original leaders move on.

## When to use it

Reach for this to lead a substantial organizational change: a new strategy, a restructuring, a merger integration, a cultural shift, a transformation. Kotter is org-level: it moves the whole organization through the change.

- If you need the individual side, who actually has the awareness, desire, knowledge, ability, and reinforcement to adopt the change, use [adkar](../adkar/); it pairs directly with Kotter, one running the org steps while the other diagnoses each person.
- If 7-S shows the parts of the organization pulling in different directions and you want to diagnose that misalignment first, use [mckinsey-7s](../mckinsey-7s/).
- If you want a lighter analytical read on the forces for and against a change before committing, use [force-field-analysis](../force-field-analysis/).
- If you want to map who must be on board and how to engage them, use [stakeholder-analysis](../stakeholder-analysis/).
